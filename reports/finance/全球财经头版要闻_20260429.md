## 📌 一、本日最大主线：美联储鲍威尔"告别会"——维持利率不变但现34年来最大分歧

> 美联储在鲍威尔担任主席的最后一次FOMC会议上维持基准利率3.50%-3.75%不变，但出现4张反对票——为1992年以来最多。声明措辞将中东局势对经济影响的表述从"不确定"升级为"高度不确定性"，并新增"高通胀部分源于全球能源价格上涨"。这场"告别会"为鲍威尔八年联储主席生涯画上了充满争议的句号。

### 1. 四票异议暴露深层分歧（WSJ/Forbes/Reuters）
- **Waller理事**：今年坚持应降息25bp，认为当前利率过度限制性
- **三位地方联储主席**（克利夫兰Hammack等）：支持维持利率不变，但反对声明保留"宽松倾向"措辞，认为在油价持续高企和供应链受阻背景下暗示降息不当
- 这是1992年11月以来FOMC出现最多反对票的一次会议，标志着美联储内部对通胀与就业的优先级判断出现根本性裂痕

### 2. 鲍威尔宣布将留任联储理事（WSJ/Forbes/USA Today）
- 鲍威尔确认5月15日主席任期届满后将继续担任联储理事
- 去留原因：司法部此前对联储大楼翻修超支的刑事调查现已撤销，但鲍威尔称"法律威胁"使他决定留在联储
- 同日参议院银行委员会通过Kevin Warsh主席提名，扫清其继任最后程序障碍
- 鲍威尔被问及对继任者建议时回答："I'm not going to give him advice."

### 3. 声明措辞变化释放通胀警示信号（Bloomberg/Reuters）
- **新增表述**："高通胀部分反映近期全球能源价格上升"——直接点名伊朗战争外溢效应
- **升级表述**：中东局势对美国经济"加剧高度不确定性"（此前仅为"不确定性"）
- **保留**："准备在适当情况下调整货币政策立场"——维持宽松倾向引发三票反对
- CME FedWatch：市场预计年内降息概率进一步下降，6月会议维持不变概率升至88%

# 📖 英文精读：Fed's Last Powell Meeting — Dissents, Oil Shocks, and the Road Ahead

> **选文理由**：美联储决议是本日最具跨源覆盖度的核心事件（六大媒体全部报道），且决议声明措辞变化和四票分歧对全球市场具有深远影响。以下综合 WSJ、Forbes、Business Insider、USA Today 等报道还原完整叙事。

## 原文精读（综合还原）

**A divided Federal Reserve held interest rates steady** `[divided: 分裂的、意见不一的——形容内部存在重大分歧]` at what is expected to be Jerome Powell's last meeting as the central bank's chair. The Federal Open Market Committee voted to keep the federal funds rate `[federal funds rate: 联邦基金利率——美联储的基准政策利率，银行间隔夜拆借利率]` in a target range of 3.50% to 3.75%, marking the third consecutive pause `[consecutive pause: 连续暂停——指连续三次会议未调整利率]` in what had been an easing cycle `[easing cycle: 宽松周期——指央行连续降息的政策阶段]` through the end of last year.

The decision came with **four dissents** `[dissent: 反对票/异议——FOMC委员对多数决定投反对票]` — the most since November 1992 `[since November 1992: 自1992年11月以来——上一次出现如此多反对票的时间]` — exposing a deepening rift `[deepening rift: 不断加深的裂痕——比喻内部分歧越来越大]` within the committee over how to navigate `[navigate: 驾驭、应对——比喻在复杂局面中找到方向]` an economy buffeted by `[buffeted by: 受…冲击——buffet作动词表示反复打击]` surging energy prices and snarled supply chains `[snarled supply chains: 纠缠受阻的供应链——snarl原意为缠结、堵塞]` stemming from the US-Israel war on Iran.

**Governor Christopher Waller cast a dovish dissent** `[dovish dissent: 鸽派反对票——主张更宽松政策的异议]`, arguing that rates remain overly restrictive `[overly restrictive: 过度限制性的——认为利率太高抑制了经济活动]` and that a 25-basis-point cut `[basis point: 基点——利率单位，1bp=0.01%]` was warranted. Three regional Fed presidents — Cleveland's Beth Hammack, Kansas City's Jeff Schmid, and Dallas's Lorie Logan — also dissented, but for a hawkish reason `[hawkish reason: 鹰派理由——主张更紧缩政策的立场]`: they supported holding rates unchanged but objected to the statement retaining language that tilts toward `[tilts toward: 倾向于——tilt表示倾斜、偏向]` future easing, arguing it was inappropriate given persistent inflation pressures `[persistent inflation pressures: 持续的通胀压力——通胀居高不下难以消退]`.

The post-meeting statement struck a notably more cautious tone `[struck a notably more cautious tone: 呈现出明显更为谨慎的基调——strike a tone为固定搭配]` on inflation, adding new language acknowledging that "elevated inflation partly reflects recent increases in global energy prices" `[elevated inflation: 高企的通胀——elevated表示处于高位的]` — a direct reference to the economic fallout `[economic fallout: 经济余波/冲击后果——fallout原指核辐射尘降，引申为负面后果]` from the Iran conflict and the Strait of Hormuz blockade `[Strait of Hormuz blockade: 霍尔木兹海峡封锁——连接波斯湾与阿曼湾的关键海上航道被封锁]`. The committee also upgraded its characterization of geopolitical risks from creating "uncertainty" to "heightened uncertainty" `[heightened uncertainty: 加剧的/高度的不确定性——heightened表示增强的]` for the economic outlook.

At a press conference following the decision, **Powell confirmed this would be his final meeting as chair** `[chair: 主席——此处指美联储主席职位]` and said he would remain on the Fed's Board of Governors `[Board of Governors: 联储理事会——美联储七人核心决策机构]` past the May 15 end of his term. Powell's decision to stay stems from `[stems from: 源于——stem from为固定短语]` the Justice Department's criminal investigation into cost overruns `[cost overruns: 成本超支——指项目费用超出预算]` on the renovation of two Fed buildings — a probe `[probe: 调查——正式的官方调查]` that had been perceived as political pressure `[perceived as political pressure: 被视为政治施压——perceived表示被认为]`. The Justice Department last week said it would end the investigation, which Powell welcomed, but he noted that "legal threats" had been a factor in his decision to remain.

When asked what advice he would give his successor `[successor: 继任者——指即将接任的Kevin Warsh]` — presumably Kevin Warsh, whose nomination advanced from the Senate Banking Committee the same morning — Powell said simply: **"I'm not going to give him advice."** `[这句简洁有力，体现了Powell一贯的克制风格]`

The decision landed `[landed: 落地、到来——比喻决策公布]` against a backdrop of `[against a backdrop of: 在…背景下——正式书面表达]` extraordinary market stress. Oil prices have surged back above $110 per barrel as the US naval blockade of Iranian ports enters its seventh week with no diplomatic breakthrough `[diplomatic breakthrough: 外交突破——指谈判取得实质性进展]` in sight. The World Bank warned earlier this week that if the most severe supply disruptions end by May, energy prices could still jump 24% for the year — and far more if hostilities escalate `[hostilities escalate: 敌对行动升级——hostilities指武装冲突]`.

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, speaking separately, said Washington's **"maximum pressure" campaign** `[maximum pressure campaign: 极限施压运动——美国对伊朗的经济制裁与军事威慑总策略]` had caused Iran's inflation to accelerate and that the country was "running out of oil storage" `[running out of oil storage: 石油储存能力告罄——无法继续储存卖不出去的原油]`. President Trump claimed on social media that Iran was in "a state of collapse" `[a state of collapse: 崩溃状态——形容经济或体制濒临瓦解]`.

For markets, the immediate takeaway was that **rate cuts remain firmly off the table** `[off the table: 不在考虑范围内——比喻某选项被排除]` for the foreseeable future `[for the foreseeable future: 在可预见的未来——常用于展望短中期前景]`. With the Warsh era set to begin — and with it, likely less communication and more opacity `[opacity: 不透明性——与transparency透明度相对]` from the central bank — Wednesday's meeting may have marked the end of an era in more ways than one `[in more ways than one: 在不止一个方面——强调多重意义]`.

| 短语 | 含义 | 用法 |

| struck a cautious tone | 呈现谨慎基调 | strike a tone=传达某种语气/态度 |
| for the foreseeable future | 在可预见的未来 | 展望短中期前景的正式表达 |

### 修辞与写作技巧

1. **对比并置(Juxtaposition)**："Governor Waller cast a dovish dissent... Three regional presidents also dissented, but for a hawkish reason" — 通过同一动词(dissent)的不同动机对比，一句话就让读者理解联储内部"左右夹击"的困境。

2. **层层递进(Escalation)**：声明措辞从 "uncertainty" 升级为 "heightened uncertainty"，记者精准捕捉这一微妙变化并以此展开叙事，体现了央行观察报道(Fed watching)的核心技巧——读懂字里行间。

3. **简洁有力的引语收尾**："I'm not going to give him advice." — 全文在Powell这句克制而意味深长的话中结束，既展示了人物性格，也为读者留下思考空间。

### 语法亮点

1. **过去分词短语作后置定语**：
   - "an economy **buffeted by** surging energy prices and snarled supply chains **stemming from** the war"
   - 两层嵌套：buffeted by修饰economy，stemming from修饰supply chains，层次清晰

2. **What引导的名词性从句**：
   - "at **what is expected to be** Jerome Powell's last meeting as chair"
   - what = the thing that，整个从句作介词at的宾语

3. **倒装强调**：
   - "With the Warsh era **set to begin** — and with it, likely less communication..."
   - "with it"承前省略，插入破折号形成补充说明，信息密度极高
