## 📌 三、Jamie Dimon 致股东信：滞胀风险与美国经济的"构造板块"

摩根大通 CEO Jamie Dimon 发布了其 2026 年度致股东信，全文提及"America"超过 80 次，但最引人注目的是**一个只出现了一次的词：stagflation（滞胀）**。（据 Fortune 报道）

**核心观点**：
- 伊朗战争和俄乌冲突可能导致**衰退**（recession），这通常会抑制通胀；但也可能导致**滞胀**（stagflation），即通胀力量压过通缩力量
- "**派对中的臭鼬——它可能在 2026 年出现——是通胀缓慢上升而非缓慢下降。仅此一项就可能导致利率上升和资产价格下跌**"
- 较大风险"**就像构造板块**"（tectonic plates），始终在移动，偶尔会在碰撞时引发地震和火山

**亮点**：Dimon 也指出了经济中的积极因素——《One Big Beautiful Bill Act》的财政刺激、AI 驱动的资本支出、放松监管政策、以及美联储的证券购买。

## 📌 四、OpenAI "AI 新政"：Sam Altman 为超级智能时代描绘社会契约

OpenAI 发布了一份 **13 页政策建议白皮书**，CEO Sam Altman 呼吁建立面向 AI 超级智能时代的"新政"（New Deal），引发巨大争议。（Bloomberg / Fortune / The Hill）

**六大核心提案**：
1. **对自动化劳动征税**：为 AI 替代的工作岗位设立税收机制
2. **公共财富基金**（Public Wealth Fund）：让每个美国人持有 AI 增长的股份
3. **四天工作制**：应对 AI 导致的工作时间缩减
4. **对富人征收巨额新税**：包括 Altman 自己这样的富人
5. **重新设计教育体系**：适应 AI 时代的人才需求
6. **超级智能安全治理框架**

**各方反应**：
- 批评者称 OpenAI 的政策意图是"**监管虚无主义**"（regulatory nihilism）的遮羞布
- 巧合的是，同一天《纽约客》发表了调查 Altman 个人可信度的深度报道
- Jamie Dimon 的四天工作制提议与 Altman 不谋而合

**Goldman Sachs 研究**为 Altman 的提案提供了数据支撑：AI 正以**每月约 16,000 个净工作岗位**的速度冲击美国就业市场，Z 世代和入门级岗位首当其冲。

## 原文标题
**'Skunk at the party': Jamie Dimon warns of recession risks amid ongoing wars—but also stagflation**

**来源**: Fortune | **作者**: Jake Angelo | **日期**: April 6, 2026

> 选文理由：Jamie Dimon 年度致股东信是华尔街最具影响力的年度文件之一，本文对其核心观点的解读用词精准、修辞丰富，适合学习金融领域的高级词汇和表达方式。

---

## 全文精读（含标注）

Jamie Dimon mentioned "America" more than 80 times in his 2026 annual letter to shareholders `[annual letter to shareholders: 年度致股东信——上市公司 CEO 每年发布的重要文件，类似于个人版的"年度国情咨文"]`, laying out goals for improving the country's infrastructure `[infrastructure: 基础设施——道路、桥梁、电网等]`, military, and the American Dream. He also mentioned one word once that could jeopardize `[jeopardize: 危及，危害——比 endanger 更正式，常用于经济/法律语境]` those very goals: "stagflation `[stagflation: 滞胀——stagnation(停滞) + inflation(通胀) 的合成词，指经济停滞与通胀同时出现的罕见组合，首次出现于 1970 年代]`."

The JPMorgan Chase CEO warned of the economic dangers of protracted `[protracted: 旷日持久的，拖延的——比 long 更强调"不情愿地持续"，常搭配 war, conflict, negotiations]` foreign conflicts, including in Iran and Russia's invasion of Ukraine. He thinks they could lead to a recession `[recession: 经济衰退——技术定义为 GDP 连续两个季度负增长]`, which would reduce inflation. But he also fears a stagflationary `[stagflationary: 滞胀性的——stagflation 的形容词形式]` outcome, combining recessionary elements with higher inflation.

"There are some scenarios that would result in a recession, which generally reduces inflation, and other scenarios that would lead to a recession with inflation (stagflation—where inflationary forces overcome deflationary `[deflationary: 通缩性的——deflation(通缩)的形容词，与 inflationary 相对]` ones)," Dimon wrote.

"The skunk at the party `[skunk at the party: 派对中的臭鼬——美国俚语，指令人不快的存在或破坏气氛的人/事物。这里 Dimon 用来比喻通胀上升可能"搞砸"经济增长的好形势]`—and it could happen in 2026—would be inflation slowly going up, as opposed to slowly going down. This alone could cause interest rates to rise and asset prices `[asset prices: 资产价格——包括股票、房地产、债券等各类资产的市场价格]` to drop."

The CEO is not alone in his fears. Several top economists have warned of stagflation—the toxic economic cocktail `[toxic economic cocktail: 有毒的经济鸡尾酒——生动的比喻，形容多种负面经济因素混合在一起的危险组合]` mixing inflation with stagnating growth and high unemployment—since the start of the Iran war in late February, especially if it stretches on `[stretch on: 拖延下去——强调时间上的持续延伸]` for several months.

The war has entered its sixth week, and there are few signs that it will end soon. President Donald Trump has demanded that Iran make a deal that opens the Strait of Hormuz `[Strait of Hormuz: 霍尔木兹海峡——位于伊朗和阿曼之间，连接波斯湾和阿拉伯海，全球约 20% 的石油通过此处运输，是世界上最重要的石油运输咽喉要道]` and allows oil prices to head back down.

But if his Tuesday 8 p.m. ET deadline `[deadline: 最后期限——这里指特朗普设定的谈判截止时间]` isn't met, he threatened to rain "hell" `[rain hell: 降下地狱般的打击——极其强硬的军事威胁用语，比 rain fire 更具末日感]` on the country, which could blow any shaky hopes of a ceasefire `[ceasefire: 停火——cease(停止) + fire(射击)]` out of the water `[blow ... out of the water: 彻底摧毁——原为海军用语，指将敌舰炸出水面，现泛指彻底击败或摧毁某事物]`.

**ECONOMIC 'EARTHQUAKES AND VOLCANOES'**

However, there's a vocal group `[vocal group: 直言不讳的群体——vocal 在此不指"声乐的"，而是"大声表态的、积极发言的"]` of economists who think fears of stagflation are unwarranted `[unwarranted: 没有根据的，不合理的——un(否定) + warranted(有正当理由的)]`. While about one-fifth of the world's oil supply passes through the Strait of Hormuz, stagflation isn't likely to come to fruition `[come to fruition: 成为现实，实现——fruition 源自拉丁语 frui(享受)，原义为"结出果实"]`, said Preston Caldwell, chief U.S. economist at financial services firm Morningstar `[Morningstar: 晨星公司——全球知名的投资研究和基金评级机构]`, calling comparisons to the 1970s "misplaced `[misplaced: 不恰当的，错位的——比 wrong 更委婉，暗示对比角度有误]`."

He pointed to the U.S.'s greater structural resilience `[structural resilience: 结构性韧性——指经济体在面对冲击时的内在恢复能力]` and reduced dependence on petroleum products `[petroleum products: 石油产品——包括汽油、柴油、航空燃油等石油衍生品]` since the 1970s, when stagflation first emerged.

"Spending on petroleum products as a share of total personal consumption `[personal consumption: 个人消费——经济学术语，指居民在商品和服务上的支出总额]` was around 3.3% in 2025, less than one-half of its 8.3% average in the 1970s," Caldwell explained.

Still, even while doubting stagflation, he predicted the personal consumption expenditures `[personal consumption expenditures (PCE): 个人消费支出指数——美联储首选的通胀衡量指标，比 CPI 更全面]` index will hit an annual rate of 3.6%, up a percentage point `[percentage point: 百分点——注意与 percent 的区别：从 2.6% 升至 3.6% 是上升了 1 个百分点(percentage point)，而非 1%]` from his initial forecast earlier this year. The Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development `[OECD (Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development): 经济合作与发展组织——由 38 个发达经济体组成的国际组织，总部在巴黎]` warned U.S. inflation could reach 4.2%.

The latest inflation report from the Bureau of Labor Statistics `[Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS): 美国劳工统计局——负责发布就业、物价等关键经济数据]` showed that the consumer price index `[consumer price index (CPI): 消费者价格指数——衡量一篮子消费品和服务价格变动的指标]` held steady `[held steady: 保持稳定——金融新闻中的常用表达]` in February at an annual rate of 2.4%. However, that number doesn't reflect price changes since the start of the Iran war.

Despite some pointed pessimism `[pointed pessimism: 尖锐的悲观——pointed 在此意为"尖锐的、直接的"，不是"被指出的"]`, Dimon also laid out `[laid out: 阐述，详细说明——lay out 的过去式，常用于正式报告和信件中]` several bright spots `[bright spots: 亮点——比喻经济中的积极因素]` buoying `[buoying: 支撑，提振——buoy 原义为"浮标"，动词意为"使浮起"，引申为"支撑/鼓舞"]` the economy, including fiscal stimulus `[fiscal stimulus: 财政刺激——政府通过减税或增加支出来提振经济的措施]` from the One Big Beautiful Bill Act; AI-driven capital spending `[capital spending: 资本支出——企业用于购买长期资产（设备、工厂等）的投资]`; deregulatory `[deregulatory: 放松管制的——de(去除) + regulatory(监管的)]` policies; and the Federal Reserve's purchases of securities `[securities: 证券——包括股票、债券等金融工具的统称]`.

But aside from the war in Iran and other geopolitical conflicts `[geopolitical conflicts: 地缘政治冲突——涉及国家间政治、军事和经济利益的冲突]`, the CEO remains wary `[wary: 警惕的，谨慎的——比 careful 更强调对潜在危险的警觉]` of other headwinds `[headwinds: 逆风——金融比喻，指阻碍经济/市场增长的因素，与 tailwinds(顺风)相对]`, including global deficits `[deficits: 赤字——政府支出超过收入的差额]`, high asset prices, and private credit `[private credit: 私人信贷——指非银行金融机构（如对冲基金、PE）提供的贷款，近年规模快速膨胀，被视为潜在风险]`.

"I think some of the larger risks are much like tectonic plates `[tectonic plates: 构造板块——地质学术语，此处 Dimon 用作比喻，形容经济风险像地壳板块一样持续运动、积累能量]`," he wrote, "always moving and periodically causing earthquakes and volcanoes when they crash into each other."

### 修辞与写作技巧

1. **数字对比增强说服力**："3.3% in 2025, less than one-half of its 8.3% average in the 1970s"——通过具体数字对比来论证"70 年代类比不成立"
2. **单词频次制造悬念**："mentioned 'America' more than 80 times ... mentioned one word once"——80 次与 1 次的对比，突出 stagflation 这个词的分量
3. **地质学比喻**："tectonic plates ... earthquakes and volcanoes"——Dimon 用地质学比喻来描述经济风险的累积和爆发，生动且具有画面感
4. **动物比喻**："skunk at the party"——将通胀人格化为"派对臭鼬"，既幽默又令人警醒
5. **"有毒鸡尾酒"比喻**："toxic economic cocktail mixing inflation with stagnating growth"——将滞胀的三个组成要素比作混合在一起的有毒饮品

### 语法亮点

1. **同位语解释**："stagflation—the toxic economic cocktail mixing inflation with stagnating growth and high unemployment—"——两个破折号之间的同位语用于解释专业术语，是新闻写作中常见的技巧
2. **括号内补充**："(stagflation—where inflationary forces overcome deflationary ones)"——直接引用中用括号补充定义
3. **让步-转折结构**："Still, even while doubting stagflation, he predicted the PCE index will hit 3.6%"——"even while + 现在分词"表达让步
4. **虚拟条件暗含**："which could blow any shaky hopes ... out of the water"——could 暗示这是对未来可能性的推测
5. **对比并列**："as opposed to slowly going down"——"as opposed to" 用于明确对比，比 "not" 更正式和精确
