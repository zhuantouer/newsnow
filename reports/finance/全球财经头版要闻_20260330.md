## 原文精读（生词/短语/语法标注版）

Trump said the Iran war was 'very complete' three weeks ago. Oil has surged `[surge: 猛涨、飙升，常用于描述价格/情绪等急剧上升]` 50% since, and analysts are warning of **stagflation** `[stagflation: 滞胀——经济停滞(stagnation)+通货膨胀(inflation)的合成词，经济学核心术语]`

---

On March 9, President Donald Trump picked up a phone call from CBS at his golf course in **Doral** `[Doral: 佛罗里达州多拉尔市，特朗普高尔夫俱乐部所在地]`, Fla., and said: "I think the war is very complete, **pretty much** `[pretty much: 差不多、基本上——非常口语化的表达，= almost/basically]`."

"Iran has no navy, no communications, they've got no air force. Their missiles are **down to a scatter** `[down to a scatter: 已经所剩无几/七零八落——scatter意为"散落、零星"，down to表示"减少到"]`. Their **drones** `[drone: 无人机]` are being blown up all over the place, including their manufacturing of drones," the president told the correspondent `[correspondent: 记者/通讯员——注意不是"通信者"，新闻语境中特指驻外记者或特约记者]`.

That was one week after the war began, when only 3,000 targets were destroyed, and markets **took Trump at his word** `[take sb at their word: 相信某人说的话，信以为真——重要习语]`, causing the price of oil to drop **a whopping** `[a whopping: 巨大的、高达——口语中强调数字之大，= an astonishing/a massive]` $13, all the way down to $91.

### A 50% surge in one month

Three weeks later, the war **has no end in sight** `[have no end in sight: 看不到尽头——常用短语，描述困境/冲突持续]`, and markets are so **numb** `[numb: 麻木的——原意指身体麻木，此处比喻市场对消息已无感]` to the president's Sunday evening/Monday morning habit of insisting **peace talks** `[peace talks: 和平谈判——固定搭配]` are happening or **walking back** `[walk back: 收回(之前的言论/立场)——重要政治新闻用语，= retract/backtrack]` previous threats to Iran in order to **calm markets** `[calm markets: 安抚市场——金融新闻中的常见搭配]` that they barely react to what he says anymore. **Brent crude** `[Brent crude: 布伦特原油——全球原油定价基准之一]` **futures** `[futures: 期货——金融术语]` for May delivery climbed to their near peak in the **futures market** `[futures market: 期货市场]` Sunday evening, before **drifting down** `[drift down: 缓慢下跌——drift有"漂移"之意，形容价格不急不徐地下滑]` to $113 on Monday.

**West Texas Intermediate** `[West Texas Intermediate (WTI): 西德克萨斯中质原油——美国原油定价基准]`, the **benchmark** `[benchmark: 基准——金融/技术领域常用词]` for American oil prices, rose to roughly $101 a barrel, as signs show that the **gas crisis** `[gas crisis: 油气危机——美式英语中gas常指汽油(gasoline)]` that has been **roiling** `[roil: 搅乱、使动荡——较正式的新闻用词，比disturb更强烈]` Asia—causing South Koreans to be told to take shorter showers; Thais to wear shorter sleeves to **conserve energy** `[conserve energy: 节约能源——固定搭配]`; and the Philippines to **distribute cash aid** `[distribute cash aid: 发放现金补贴]` to motorcyclists **slammed by** `[slammed by: 受到重创——slam原义是"猛击"，此处引申为"严重受到影响"]` higher fuel costs—is **far from contained** `[far from contained: 远未被控制住——far from + adj表示"远非…"，contained = 被遏制的]`. The same **supply disruptions** `[supply disruption: 供应中断——经济新闻核心词组]` driving those Asian measures are now pushing American gas prices to three-year highs. Brent has now soared more than 50% in March, putting it **on track for** `[on track for: 有望达到/正朝着…的方向发展——常用短语]` the **steepest monthly gain** `[steepest monthly gain: 最大月度涨幅——steep在金融语境中形容涨跌幅度大]` since the 1990 Gulf War.

### The war is widening, not winding down

`[winding down: 逐渐减弱/接近尾声——wind down是非常重要的短语动词，与wind up(结束/紧张)相对]`

Meanwhile, all signs point to the war **escalating on multiple fronts** `[escalate on multiple fronts: 在多条战线上升级——escalate = 升级，front = 战线/前线]`. Yemen's Iran-backed **Houthi rebels** `[Houthi rebels: 胡塞武装叛军——也门反政府武装]` entered the war over the weekend after weeks of silence, launching **cruise missiles** `[cruise missile: 巡航导弹]` and drones at Israel. The Pentagon is **reportedly** `[reportedly: 据报道——新闻写作高频词，表示信息来自消息源而非直接确认]` preparing for weeks of **ground operations** `[ground operations: 地面作战行动]` inside Iran, according to the _Wall Street Journal_, including a potentially **devastating** `[devastating: 毁灭性的——比destructive更强烈的词]` and dangerous mission to **excavate uranium** `[excavate uranium: 挖掘/提取铀——excavate本义为"挖掘/发掘"，考古中也常用]` from the country. And in an early Monday post on Truth Social, Trump threatened "blowing up and completely **obliterating**" `[obliterate: 彻底摧毁/消灭——语气极强的动词，比destroy更文学化]` Iran's power plants, oil wells, and **Kharg Island export hub** `[Kharg Island: 哈尔克岛——伊朗最重要的原油出口枢纽]` if a deal isn't reached and the **Strait of Hormuz** `[Strait of Hormuz: 霍尔木兹海峡——全球最重要的石油运输通道]` isn't immediately reopened.

The consequences are already **slamming** American consumers. The national average gas price hit $3.99 on Monday, up from $2.98 in February, according to AAA—the highest since the crisis caused by Russia's **invasion** `[invasion: 入侵]` of Ukraine in 2022. Wall Street is now **bracing for** `[brace for: 为…做好准备——常用于描述面对坏消息/冲击时的心理准备，= prepare for (something bad)]` the inevitable **second-order effects** `[second-order effects: 二阶效应——指间接连锁反应，非直接后果]`. **Société Générale** `[Société Générale: 法国兴业银行]` analysts wrote Monday that they expect "**higher for longer**" `[higher for longer: 更高更久——金融术语，原本用于描述利率政策，此处用于油价]` Brent prices, forecasting a **base case** `[base case: 基准情景——金融分析中的"基本预测"，相对于bull case(乐观)/bear case(悲观)]` of $125 on average in April, with "**credible spikes**" `[credible spikes: 可信的价格飙升——credible = 可信的，spike = 骤然上涨]` toward $150 if the **Bab el-Mandeb Strait** `[Bab el-Mandeb Strait: 曼德海峡——红海南端的关键航运通道]` at the southern end of the Red Sea is shut down by the now entering Houthi forces.

### Wall Street's stagflation fears are growing

Analysts are currently **agonizing over** `[agonize over: 为…苦恼/纠结——比worry about更强烈，有"痛苦挣扎"之意]` whether or not to "**look through**" `[look through: 看穿/无视(短期波动)——投资术语，意为"忽略暂时性因素，关注长期趋势"]` the potential **inflation shock** `[inflation shock: 通胀冲击]` from higher oil prices, as many **cling to** `[cling to: 紧抱/执着于——比hold on to更有绝望感，形容不愿放弃某种希望]` hopes that the Fed will cut rates. Inflation has "**flatlined**" `[flatline: 持平/停滞不动——原义是心电图变成平线(死亡)，此处形容通胀率停在一个水平不动]` at 3% for the past couple of years, Jim McCormick, **chief global macro strategist** `[chief global macro strategist: 首席全球宏观策略师——华尔街常见头衔]` at Citi, told Bloomberg TV, and now with the added risks from higher **commodity prices** `[commodity prices: 大宗商品价格]`, it's looking like inflation could be "**significantly higher** in the coming months."

Chair of the Federal Reserve Jerome Powell said during a Q&A at Harvard University on Monday that the Fed hadn't **lost sight of** `[lose sight of: 忽视/看不到——重要习语，此处指美联储没有忘记其通胀目标]` its 2% inflation target, but that the Fed's tools have "**no meaningful effect on supply shocks**" `[supply shock: 供给冲击——经济学术语，指突然的供给减少导致价格飙升]`. Meaning the Fed can't rescue markets or consumers from rising gas prices or the resulting price hikes in groceries and other items. McCormick was clear that this was not an environment for investors to **take on more risk** `[take on risk: 承担风险——金融常用搭配]`.

"The mix we're looking at, which seems quite obvious now, is more stagflation," he added. "Growth is going to be **marked down** `[mark down: 下调——金融语境中指分析师下调经济预测]` as a result of this conflict; inflation is going to be **marked up** `[mark up: 上调——与mark down相对]`. It's not great for bonds. It's not great for equities `[equities: 股票/权益——金融术语，= stocks]`. It's a pretty bad mix for markets **in general** `[in general: 总体而言——常用过渡短语]`."

In the sixth week of this conflict, there's a bit of a "**can't do this anymore**" sense among investors, exhausted by the volatile `[volatile: 波动剧烈的——金融高频词，形容价格大起大落]` ups and downs and waiting for the **doomsday** `[doomsday: 末日——此处形容最悲观的预测场景]` $200 oil projections `[projection: 预测/预期——比forecast更正式]` to set in. Economist Ed Yardeni told clients over the weekend that this "**fetal position**" `[fetal position: 胎儿姿势——非常生动的比喻，形容投资者像受惊的胎儿一样蜷缩防御，= 极度防御/退缩状态]` investors are retreating into is a sign that Trump is at least not **bluffing** `[bluff: 虚张声势——扑克术语引申而来，此处指"不是在说空话"]` about escalating the conflict.

### 修辞与写作技巧
1. **双关语(Pun)**: "fog of war" → "bog of war" — 将经典军事术语改一个字母，变成"战争泥潭"，极其巧妙
2. **比喻(Metaphor)**: "fetal position" — 用胎儿蜷缩的姿势比喻投资者的极度防御心态
3. **对比结构**: "Growth is going to be marked down; inflation is going to be marked up" — 工整的平行结构，一上一下形成强烈对比
4. **语气递进**: numb → barely react → can't do this anymore → fetal position — 描述市场情绪从麻木到崩溃的递进

### 语法亮点
1. **so...that...结构**: "markets are so numb to...that they barely react" — so + adj + that引导结果状语从句
2. **分词作状语**: "causing South Koreans to be told to..." — 现在分词短语作结果状语
3. **虚拟条件**: "if a deal isn't reached" — 真实条件句，用一般现在时表示将来可能发生的条件
